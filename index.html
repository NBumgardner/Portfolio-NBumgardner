Online Games and HCI

This is my most recent team project. As a team of five programmers we created two online games and a website to host them on Microsoft Azure. We gathered analytics by measuring how long users kept our website tabs open or in focus. We used this HCI data to compare the games' engagement levels.

In development, we used an Agile-Scrum work method and multiple technologies to complete the project (Jan. 2016 - May 2016). Floe, one of the two games, has its source code on GitHub.

  • Deliverables: HCI report, website, and two online games.
  • Primary Language: HaxePunk
  • Team: Five student programmers in Software Engineering at MSU.
  • My Main Roles: Tester, Music Composer, Licenser, Organizer.
  • Additional Roles: Scrum Master, Programmer, Website Editor.

This project was possible by the efforts of a great team of people. It was challenging and completed early.
